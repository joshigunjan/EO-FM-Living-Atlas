Method

How entries are structured and verified.

Scope

The atlas focuses on Earth observation and remote-sensing foundation models, including visual, multimodal, SAR/radar, spectral/hyperspectral, Earth-system, and vision-language/MLLM models. Benchmarks and datasets are tracked separately in the watchlist until converted into full structured entries.

What makes this different from an awesome list?

An awesome list usually records abbreviation, title, paper, code, and weights. This atlas keeps those links but adds scientific structure: scope, modalities, architecture family, downstream tasks, training scale, openness, caveats, and review status.

Status labels

Curated seed means the row comes from the manually curated seed workbook and is treated as part of the initial verified catalogue. Specialized seed means the row is included from the seed workbook but belongs to a specialized subgroup, usually vision-language or MLLM models. Candidate means a submitted or automatically discovered entry that still needs human review.

Link policy

Rows should link to original papers, project pages, code repositories, and model weights, not just to another registry. External registries such as Awesome Remote Sensing Foundation Models are used as provenance and cross-checking sources for paper/code/weight links.

Minimum fields for a verified row

  • Model name and paper title
  • Original paper link
  • Scope and model category
  • Input modalities and sensors where available
  • Architecture family
  • Named downstream tasks
  • Openness: code, weights, data, or closed/unclear
  • Notes and caveats
